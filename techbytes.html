Project Description

This was a 12 weeks project in a third-year level course for interaction design methods. We were given the task to pick a group to study through interviews, workshops, and role-playing to come up with a design focus and design a mock-up of the final concept. My partner and I came up with a note-taking and planner application called TechDrive for TechBytes mentors.

Process

The major roles I played in this project were prototyping and designing the flow of TechDrive using and creating small workshops for the mentors to learn more about the culture and "language" of the participant group. I created the prototype that's clickable on the phone view (portrait) with Axure RP as well as the desktop view (landscape) on Fluidui. Making the phone view as well as the desktop view required me to rearrange or add/remove parts of the applications that were more or less important for the mentor when using a certain device. The workshops were intended for the mentors to experience and engage in activities together rather than a plain individual questionnaire or survey.

Outcome

From this project, I gained valuable knowledge of design process and design thinking. The interaction and engagement of the end users through the workshops we designed allowed us to get to know what the mentors needed in order to build on the success for the group rather than just being a want factor. Overall, we believe the concept of this either can be expanded to other groups for sharing meeting notes and not just for TechBytes mentors.

When my partner and I pitched our idea to the TechBytes mentor, they liked the concept of the idea and believe that TechDrive provides a backbone especially to new mentors. TechDrive allowed for each mentor to refer back to past notes for help and have more time to practise and be more confident in presenting.
