Project Description

Being part of the UX Team, we were tasked to re-design the Surrey Public Wi-fi landing page. The requirements were flexible as long as it was responsive and user-friendly. We also needed to create the design so that it can also be implemented for the Surrey Library Wi-fi which requires consistency.

My Contribution

I had designed the overall visual of the landing page (except for the photographing the image) on your right. I also created mock-up version through Axure to get the overall interaction and flow to outside developers and the presentation slides to explain our proposed options.

Process

First we approached this project by analyzing the current page and the overall user experience of it.The page is not responsive. User would need to zoom in to check off the Terms & Agreement and press the button to proceed. Checkboxes are not user-friendly on mobile devices. There’s no consistency between the page and the City of Surrey’s branding (ex. colour). A lot of unnecessary text that’s not needed and just a space-filler.


We then analyzed what exists and the user experience of those sites. We noticed that for both Starbucks and Tim Hortons’ Wifi on Android phones, the browser automatically pops-up when the user connects to the network. We liked that they both have buttons to press and no checkmarks. Starbucks took more of a quick and simple approach which also included a way of advertising. Tim Hortons’ was more lengthy in text and time.



As a team we finalized our wireframes and mock-ups through Axure and ended up with 2 options. Option A (which my colleague Kaveh H had designed) was a dynamic slide out page. Whereas I designed Option B, the modal effect. My justification for option B is that it’s more natural to users since it’s a feature effect that has been seen in other sites. Also it’s less awkward for the eye on desktop in displaying extra information such as events.



At the end, we pitched both our ideas and finalized with Option A as the first option and B if A is not possible.

Outcome

As a result, the finalized design for the Surrey Public Wifi is responsive, quick, and easy to access for users of all ages. I as a designer had been more proactive in making sure the quality of the product produced is up to par. Therefore I would test the link provided by Datavalet and provide modification specs through the emulator that worked with all browser size.
